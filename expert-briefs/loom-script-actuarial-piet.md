# Loom script — Actuarial (Piet Maree), ~2.5–3 min

*Record over the AI Actuary page, then the Model Studio (Actuarial tab), then a Simulator run.*

**[0:00 — what it is]**
> "Hey Piet — thanks for doing this. Quick context, then exactly what I need. This is HealthID, an insurer cockpit. It ranks incentive plays by **present-valued, risk-adjusted, *verified* value** and tells an insurer where to spend. The whole pitch is that it's an actuarial tool an actuary can trust — so I need your eyes on the engine."

**[0:30 — the value chain]**
> "The chain is: reward drives engagement, engagement drives a behaviour/dose change, that maps to a **claims delta** and a **mortality margin**, present-valued and graded by how verifiable the outcome is — then optimised across cohorts under Monte-Carlo uncertainty. Your job is the valuation half: the deltas, the haircuts, and whether the methodology holds up."

**[1:00 — show Model Studio Actuarial tab]**
> *Open Model Studio → Actuarial.* "Every assumption's here with a source and a confidence flag, and there are two models — a conservative **Evidence Floor** and an optimistic **Forward** case. I want your numbers in both, and then your sign-off turns the floor into a governed set."

**[1:30 — the ask]**
> "The sheet I'm sending has the specifics, but the high-leverage asks are:
> One — the **claims bridge per signal**: annual claims delta per treated member, applicable prevalence, and — the big one — the **attribution factor**, the causal share versus selection. That single number swings the whole ROI.
> Two — **mortality and life economics**: baseline mortality, the mortality margin and its attribution, sum assured, premium, lapse improvement, LTV.
> Three — **money and time**: discount rate, valuation horizon, persisted-savings years, reward cost ratio after breakage and co-funding."

**[2:10 — the methodology ask]**
> "And the most valuable hour: tell me where the **methodology** is wrong. Is net-of-cost ROI the right headline? Are the value pools — claims, mortality, retention, acquisition — double-counting? Is the verification multiplier, where a verified outcome is worth about four times a self-reported one, defensible? What would an actuary on the other side of the table reject?"

**[2:40 — close]**
> "For each number: a value, a range, a source, and floor-versus-forward where they differ. If you only do one thing — give me a defensible attribution factor for the top signals and tell me whether the ROI logic survives scrutiny. Appreciate it, Piet — this is the part that makes it real."
